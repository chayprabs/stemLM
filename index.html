THE PROBLEM

AI gives answers.Students need understanding.

Every engineering student uses AI today. But the experience is fundamentally broken for STEM. These tools are built for general questions — not for the step-by-step visual reasoning that physics, circuits, and chemistry require.

No intermediate diagrams

The AI says "after combining R1 and R2 in parallel…" but never shows what the circuit looks like at that moment. You're left mentally constructing the thing you came to understand.

Steps get skipped

AI jumps from problem statement to near-final answer, cutting the 3–4 intermediate stages where students actually get confused. No textbook does this.

No domain awareness

A circuit problem, an optics question, and a mechanics FBD each need a completely different solution structure. AI treats them all as unstructured prose.

Solutions you can't use

Once the answer is in the chat, you can't download it, select a step and ask about it, or save it for revision. It's a block of text that disappears into history.

HOW IT WORKS

One button.Your AI gets a whole lot smarter.

1

Type your question into any AI

Works on ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini — any web-based AI. stemLM sits invisibly as a Chrome extension, like Grammarly.

Platform-agnostic
2

Press the stemLM button, then submit

A small button beside the chat input activates stemLM mode. The extension silently injects a 2–3KB instruction file alongside your question before it reaches the AI.

One tap activation
3

stemLM identifies the exact topic

The injected file calls the stemLM API, which holds a knowledge tree: Subject → Chapter → Topic → Subtopic. Each subtopic has a unique key and a step-by-step framework.

PHY → Electrostatics → RC Circuits → PHY_EL_RC_001
4

The AI follows the playbook

The AI reads the question, matches it to the exact subtopic key, retrieves the framework, and outputs a structured response — not freeform prose. The unique key is embedded in the output.

5

The extension panel opens automatically

The stemLM panel opens on the right of your screen, taking 40% of the width. It watches the response as it streams in.

6

Step-by-step, with diagrams at every stage

Each step appears as a clean card: the explanation, the formula in proper notation, and the diagram showing the circuit or FBD exactly as it looks at that moment — not the final state. Mid-solution.

7

Interact, ask follow-ups, export

Select any step and ask the AI about just that piece. Download the full solution as a clean PDF. Copy any diagram. The solution is a living document.

DIAGRAMS AT EVERY STEP

Like LaTeX for equations —but for circuits, FBDs, and ray diagrams

LaTeX solved math rendering in text. stemLM solves the same problem for STEM diagrams. Every step of your solution gets the right diagram — rendered live, in sync with the explanation, not just dumped at the end.

Step 1
Original circuit
R1 = 6Ω · R2 = 3Ω · R3 = 4Ω · V = 12V
Step 2
R1 ∥ R2 collapsed
R_p = (6 × 3) / (6 + 3) = 18/9 = 2Ω
Step 3
Final equivalent
R_eq = R3 + R_p = 4 + 2 = 6Ω
Total current: I = 12/6 = 2A
